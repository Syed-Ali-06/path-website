University Applications: Personal Statement Guidance

UK personal statements have changed. Instead of one long essay, you now answer three focused questions — but your goal remains the same: tell one clear, cohesive story.

Question 1: The Motivation

Why do you want to study this course or subject?

Identify a clear academic theme that genuinely motivates you and build your application around it. Avoid generic statements — be specific and intentional.

Question 2: Academic Preparedness

How have your studies and qualifications helped prepare you?

Focus on super-curriculars: books, lectures, competitions, or research done beyond the syllabus. Explain impact, not just activity.

Question 3: Practical Preparation

What other practical experiences have prepared you?

Show how you’ve applied your interests in the real world — projects, volunteering, work experience, or leadership roles — and link them back to your course.

Top Tips for Success

  • Create one clear narrative across all three questions
  • Don’t overthink academic vs practical categories
  • If it’s relevant and meaningful, include it

Course-Specific Guidance

Select a subject area to see tailored advice.

Law

  • Discuss legal debates, ethics, or justice
  • Reference case law or legal reading
  • Mooting and debating are highly valuable

Computer Science

  • Personal coding projects are essential
  • Explain how and why you built something
  • Mention algorithms or optimisation

Medicine

  • Reflect deeply on work experience
  • Show empathy and ethical awareness
  • Link experiences to NHS values

Engineering

  • Discuss design or problem-solving projects
  • Apply maths and physics concepts
  • Show logical and iterative thinking

University Applications: Personal Statement Guidance

UK personal statements have changed. Instead of one long essay, you now answer three focused questions — but your goal remains the same: tell one clear, cohesive story.

Question 1: The Motivation

Why do you want to study this course or subject?

This is the foundation of your application. Identify a clear academic theme that genuinely motivates you and build your application around it.

Avoid generic statements. Be specific, thoughtful, and intentional.

Question 2: Academic Preparedness

How have your studies and qualifications helped prepare you?

This section is about what you’ve done beyond the syllabus — your super-curriculars. Focus on how independent study has shaped your thinking.

Don’t list subjects. Explain impact and insight.

Question 3: Practical Preparation

What other practical experiences have prepared you?

Show how you’ve applied your interests in the real world through projects, volunteering, work experience, or leadership roles.

Always connect experiences back to skills required for your course.

Top Tips for Success

  • Create a clear narrative flow across all three questions
  • Don’t stress the difference between academic and practical
  • If it’s relevant and meaningful, include it
